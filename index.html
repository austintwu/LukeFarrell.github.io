Hi There !

    I’m Kristina Smith! I’m from Normal, Illinois, and I’m a junior majoring in Public Policy, with an interest in social policy, minoring in Education, and getting a certificate in Human Rights. I currently serve as the VP of Services and Sustainability in DSG, where I have had the opportunity to engage with the issues that affect us everyday. In my time here, I have learned that Duke, on its own, will not provide everything that we as an undergraduate body need— not without a push from student leaders. I want to be DSG president because I want to serve Duke, and I want to be one of the student leaders who pushes for these changes...

    As your DSG President, I plan to prioritize affordability and accessibility on this campus, because I care about the unnecessary barriers that some students face. I want to challenge the unhealthy parts of our campus culture. I want to broaden the reach that Duke students have not just in the world, but in our local community. I know I can achieve these goals because I have a body of project work that was inspired by student voices. During my tenure, I worked towards the creation of Daily Devil Deals which makes eating on campus more affordable for all students and staff members by installing $5 meals at every campus vendor. I collaborated with the Duke Disability Alliance to ban the Languages building as a meeting space for SOFC recognized student groups because it is the only non wheelchair accessible building on our campus. I also reached out to farmers vendors in the Durham community to bring a Farmer’s Market to K-Ville and bridge the divide between campus and the city in a tangible way.

    As your president, I will listen to you, and I will learn from you, and I will instigate lasting, positive, and concrete change for you… and that starts here with my campaign! I have collaborated with student leaders across campus to come up with my platform and I want to hear your thoughts. Please read through my policy proposals and feel free to reach out under the Contact Me tab with questions or suggestions. I hope to meet with all of you in the coming weeks to hear about your vision on how we can build a better Duke.

    LOVE,

    Kristina Smith

Daily Devil Deals

Financial aid opened the door for me to come to Duke, but I and many of my peers have struggled to stay afloat throughout my time here. A common example of the uncalculated cost of college is food insecurity— eating on Duke’s campus is unaffordable for way too many students. I took action and worked with Duke Dining to make sure that every single vendor on campus has a full lunch option that is $5. This new program has made meal-time less stressful for many students.

-Kristina Smith

Farmer’s Market on Campus

Some of my best moments at Duke have been with friends over food— meals are central to how I think about community. Last year I worked with several farmers and local vendors in the Durham community to bring a Farmer’s Market to K-Ville. I am excited to continue these relationships to bring fresh, local food to students and bridge the divide between campus and the city in a tangible way!

-Kristina

Food Truck Fair

For the past two years, I worked with my committee in DSG to allocate $10,000 to bring 6-8 local food trucks to campus for the annual Food Truck Fair. We worked with Parking and Transportation Services to reserve the space and with Duke Student Dining Advisory Committee (DUSDAC) to coordinate the trucks, publicize the event, and run it the day of.

-Kristina

Languages Building Access

I worked with the Duke Disability Alliance and SOFC to ban the Languages building as a meeting space for recognized student groups because it is the only non wheelchair accessible building on our campus. Students should not be excluded from attending events on this campus because of their ability status.

-Kristina

Lowered FLEX Increments

Previously students could only add FLEX points to their accounts in increments of $25. This was a major burden for many students- some even said that they couldn’t do laundry without loading $25 to their accounts. I worked with OIT and the DukeCard office to transform this and now students can reload accounts in increments of $10, $15, and $20.

-Kristina

Confronted Admin over Uber Restrictions

In 2017, the university placed a restriction on ride-sharing services, no longer allowing them to pick up students from the Chapel and the Bryan Center circle. Many students were upset because this would make riding inconvenient and even worse, unsafe. I pushed back against administrators and now students can get rides from these popular locations after 8pm and on weekends.

-Kristina

Reusable To-Go Containers

Duke Dining might be #1 for its food, but we’re far from the top when it comes to sustainability. As VP, I’m working to make West Union environmentally friendly by reducing the waste generated by disposable to-go containers. Beginning in March, administrators will be piloting a program where students can access reusable to-go containers.

-Kristina

Negotiated Career Closet

I recently turned in a proposal to President Price and Provost Kornbluth that would allocate nearly $100,000 a year for students on full financial aid to purchase professional clothing. Having access to business attire should not be a barrier to Duke students and I am pushing for this funding to make sure that all students have a chance to be competitive in the workforce and interviews.

-Kristina

Duke Student Government

I have proudly served on DSG’s Services and Sustainability (SAS) Committee throughout my entire Duke career. I am currently the Vice President of SAS, and I am excited for the opportunity to continue working to serve you in the coming year.

-Kristina Smith

Duke University Student Dining Advisory Committee

As the Vice President of Services and Sustainability, I have had the great pleasure of working closely with the Duke University Student Dining Advisory Committee (DUSDAC) on projects such as the Daily Devil Deals and the Food Truck Fair. This committee works intimately with Duke Dining administrators in order to address any concerns that students have with dining options and availability.

-Kristina

The President’s Council on Black Affairs

The President’s Council on Black Affairs was created to help ensure that the university pursues its goals of increasing the diversity of the student body and guaranteeing an excellent experience to its students.

-Kristina

Project Build

ProjectBuild is an eight day pre-orientation program aimed at fostering community among first-year students. pBUILD connects first-years with the Durham community through community service while also providing them with the opportunity to connect with upperclassmen crew leaders. I have served as crew leader for pBUILD for two years and I look forward to the opportunity to serve for a third year.

-Kristina

The Bridge

The Bridge is an online publication that seeks to build a community for Black and Latina women through cross-cultural and cross-campus collaborations. The Bridge aims to celebrate identities by allowing Black and Latina women to reclaim agency over their narrative through creative expression. I have been a content creator for the Bridge since its conception in the Fall of 2016.

-Kristina

Internal Affairs Diversity Working Group

The Internal Affairs Diversity Working Group was conceived in order to ensure that underrepresented voices have a place in DSG. The aim of this working group is to provide DSG members with the confidence to find their voice among their peers in DSG.

-Kristina

Center for Race Relations

The Center for Race Relations (CRR) is a student run organization dedicated to changing the face of race relations at Duke University. The CRR puts on monthly events aimed at engaging students in conversations about race. I have been involved with the CRR since my first year at Duke, and now serve as the Vice President of this organization.

-Kristina

Common Ground

Common Ground is a student-led retreat through the Center for Race Relations that explores personal identity and how it affects our daily experiences. I served as co-director of the retreat for two semesters, and I now serve as a facilitator.
